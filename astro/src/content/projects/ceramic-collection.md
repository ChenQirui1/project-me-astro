## About the Collection

This collection of ceramic vessels represents a year-long exploration of form, texture, and glaze chemistry. Each piece is hand-thrown on the wheel and finished with custom-developed glazes.

![Ceramic vessels on display](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1610701596007-11502861dcfa?w=800&h=600&fit=crop)

## Process & Technique

### Throwing

Each vessel begins as a centered ball of stoneware clay on the potter's wheel. The forms are thrown in one session to maintain consistency and flow.

![Potter's wheel in action](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1565193566173-7a0ee3dbe261?w=800&h=600&fit=crop)

### Glazing

The glazes are inspired by coastal colors - seafoam greens, sandy beiges, and deep ocean blues. Each glaze is tested extensively to achieve the desired color and texture.

## Material Exploration

Working with high-fire stoneware clay allows for durability and a beautiful natural finish. The clay body itself becomes part of the aesthetic, visible through the translucent glazes.

![Close-up of glaze texture](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1493106819501-66d381c466f1?w=800&h=600&fit=crop)

## Exhibition

The collection was featured in the "Contemporary Ceramics" exhibition at the Craft Museum, where three pieces were acquired for the permanent collection.

### Artist Statement

"These vessels are meant to be used and lived with. They bring a sense of calm and connection to natural elements into everyday life."
